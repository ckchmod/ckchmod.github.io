<p><li>When it comes to today's colleges and their love for buzz phrases such as "fostering diversity" is truly deceitful and misleading. We should call them for what they truly are. Diversity in college is simply a reverse form of racism, where you value/devalue students based on the color of their skin. Asian students benefit the least in terms of college entrance. Blacks and Hispanics benefit the most. It's affirmative action based on your phenotype, and this would be the very type of bias, whether well-intentioned or not, Dr. Martin Luther King would have been against. What did Dr. King say? "I have a dream that my four little children will one day live in a nation where they will not be judged by the color of their skin, but by the content of their character." He would be turning in his grave, if he saw what was going on in college campuses' crazy wave of intersectionality and pseudo-diversity. Call spade and spade. Diversity on campus is an action based on race, and it's harmful. Try diversity of thought for a change.</li></p>
<p><li>In this very well written <a href="https://aeon.co/essays/why-religious-identities-are-not-immune-to-robust-criticism">article</a> by professor of philosophy, Paul Russell at University of British Columbia, he makes a distinction between religious identity and human identity (which are phenotypes such as race, gender, sexual orientation that you have no control over).</li></p>
<p><li>This all goes in hand with the theme of regressive left intersectionality.</li></p>
<p><li>I truly believe that we have made so much progress in social acceptance in the last couple decades, and we've been spoiled, living in the world's leading country in almost every facet of life, that we are becoming blind to the order of priority in terms of human rights. As Dave Rubin once said, we have become so tolerant that we have become tolerant of the intolerant. Hopefully, I can write more on this topic</p></li>
